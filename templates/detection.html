Detection Methodology

Spotting the difference between artificial generation and human expression

Symmetry and Edge Artifacts

AI models generate images through probabilistic sampling, often leading to structural inconsistencies that human eyes overlook but algorithms can quantify:

  • Frequency Analysis: AI-generated images often exhibit distinct "checkerboard" artifacts in the frequency domain due to the upsampling layers in Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs).
  • Geometric Incoherence: Models struggle with complex human anatomy (such as fingers or joints) and long-range spatial dependencies, resulting in warped perspectives or "impossible" geometry.
  • Typography Errors: AI lacks a semantic understanding of written language, frequently producing nonsensical text or corrupted character glyphs within an image.
  • Lighting & Shadow Mismatch: Inconsistencies in light source orientation and shadow casting often betray an AI composite, as the model may assign shadows from multiple conflicting light sources.
  • Micro-texture Anomalies: AI-generated surfaces often exhibit "blurrier" or overly smooth high-frequency textures compared to the organic, stochastic noise naturally present in real-world photographs.
